## II. Renewal Laplacian Definition

The central quantity is the Laplacian of renewal density:

```math
∇²ρ_R
```

Interpretation:

- Positive ∇²ρ_R → coherence flows *into* the region (accumulation)  
- Negative ∇²ρ_R → coherence flows *out of* the region (dispersion)  
- Zero ∇²ρ_R → local harmonic equilibrium  

The Laplacian measures the curvature of renewal density.

## III. The Renewal Laplacian Equation

Renewal density evolves according to the diffusion-like equation:

```math
∂ρ_R/∂t = D_R · ∇²ρ_R
```

Where:

- D_R is the **renewal diffusivity coefficient**  
  (how quickly renewal propagates through the field)

Interpretation:

- High D_R → rapid renewal equalization (fast healing, fast insight flow)  
- Low D_R → slow equalization (stagnant or isolated renewal regions)

This is the Renewal Field’s **diffusion law**.

## VI. Stability Condition

Stable renewal diffusion requires:

```math
0 < D_R ≤ D_max
```

Where D_max is determined by:

- field coherence  
- operator balance (O, R, D, Χ)  
- proximity to phase boundaries  
- local renewal density  

If D_R is above threshold:

- coherence diffuses too rapidly → loss of structure  
- renewal becomes “washed out”

If D_R is below threshold:

- insufficient smoothing → persistent distortions  
- stagnation or moral/cognitive bottlenecks occur

Balanced D_R produces **healthy, harmonic propagation**.
